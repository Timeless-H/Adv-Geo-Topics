Culture and Lifestyle

Ghana is a multiethnic country with a population of Approximately 31 million as of 2020. The largest ethnic group is the Ashanti people. In 2010, the population of Ghana was 72.2% Christian (24.3% Pentecostal, 18.4% Protestant, 13.1% Catholic and 11.4% other). Approximately 18.6% of the population of Ghana are Muslim. Just over 10,000 Ghanaians practice Hinduism, with most of them being indigenous converts.

Food and Drink

Ghanaian cuisine and gastronomy is diverse, and includes an assortment of soups and stews with varied seafoods and most Ghanaian soups are prepared with vegetables, meat, poultry or fish.[243] Fish is important in the Ghanaian diet with tilapia, roasted and fried whitebait, smoked fish and crayfish all being common components of Ghanaian dishes.

Music and Dance

Ghanaian dance is as diverse as its music, and there are traditional dances and different dances for different occasions.[258] The most known Ghanaian dances are those for celebrations. These dances include the Adowa, Kpanlogo, Azonto, Klama, Agbadza, Borborbor and Bamaya. There are other modern afro musics and dances like Azonto, Alkayida, Akwaaba, just to name a few.

Sports

The national men's football team is known as the Black Stars, with the under-20 team known as the Black Satellites. Ghana has won the African Cup of Nations four times, the FIFA U-20 World Cup once, and has participated in three consecutive FIFA World Cups in 2006, 2010, and 2014. In the 2010 FIFA World Cup, Ghana became the third African country to reach the quarter-final stage of the World Cup. Ghana is the first and only country on the Africa continent to be crowned FIFA U-20 World Cup Champions. The country has also produced a number of world class boxers, including Azumah Nelson a three-time world champion and considered as Africa's greatest boxer, Nana Yaw Konadu also a three-time world champion, Ike Quartey, and Joshua Clottey.
